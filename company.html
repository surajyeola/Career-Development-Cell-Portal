Selection Process

Step 1

Aptitude

The aptitude round assesses candidates' problem-solving, analytical, and quantitative skills through a series of standardized tests. These tests may include questions on math, logic, and reasoning, designed to evaluate candidates' ability to think critically and solve problems under pressure.

Step 2

Group Discussion

candidates who perform well proceed to the group discussion round. Here, they engage in structured discussions on a given topic with other candidates, demonstrating their communication, teamwork, and persuasion skills. Group discussions allow recruiters to assess candidates' interpersonal skills, leadership potential, and ability to articulate ideas effectively.

Step 3

Interview

Interviews may be conducted by a panel of interviewers and can include technical, behavioral, and situational questions. Recruiters evaluate candidates' qualifications, experience, and suitability for the role during the interview, as well as their ability to communicate effectively and demonstrate their potential value to the organization.
