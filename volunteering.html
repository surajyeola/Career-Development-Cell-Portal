Training and Placement Cell Volunteering

Criteria Eligibility

Applicants must meet the following criteria to be eligible for volunteering:

Be a current student of the college.
Have a GPA of at least 7.5.
Be willing to commit at least 5 hours per week to volunteering activities.

Roles and Responsibilities:
Training Placement Officer

Responsibilities include:

Facilitating communication between the college and potential employers.
Organizing and coordinating placement drives, interviews, and recruitment processes.
Providing career guidance and counseling to students.
Maintaining and updating placement records and statistics.
Collaborating with industry professionals to understand job market trends and requirements.

Coordinator

Responsibilities include:

Overseeing and managing the overall volunteering program.
Coordinating activities and events related to volunteering initiatives.
Acting as a liaison between volunteers, college administration, and external stakeholders.
Delegating tasks and responsibilities to volunteers based on their skills and availability.
Ensuring effective communication and collaboration among volunteers.

Student Coordinator

Responsibilities include:

Engaging and motivating fellow students to participate in volunteering activities.
Organizing and leading student volunteer teams for specific initiatives or events.
Acting as a point of contact for student volunteers, addressing their concerns and providing support.
Assisting in planning and executing volunteering projects and campaigns.
Representing the student body in coordinating with college authorities for volunteering opportunities.
